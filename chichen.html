Chichen Itza, Mexico

Introduction

Chichen Itza is one of the most famous and well-preserved archaeological sites of the ancient Mayan civilization, located in the Yucatan Peninsula of Mexico. It was once a major city and is now one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. The site is known for its massive pyramid, El Castillo, which served as a temple to the Mayan god Kukulkan.

History

Chichen Itza was founded around the 5th century and became a powerful city by the 10th century. It was abandoned around the 15th century, likely due to political and environmental factors. The city was rediscovered in the early 19th century and has since become one of the most visited and studied ancient sites in the world. El Castillo (the Temple of Kukulkan) is perhaps the most iconic structure at Chichen Itza, designed to align with astronomical events, particularly the spring and autumn equinoxes.

Fun Facts

  • The name "Chichen Itza" means "At the mouth of the well of Itza" in the Mayan language.
  • The pyramid of El Castillo has 365 steps, symbolizing the days of the year.
  • During the equinox, the shadow of the serpent god Kukulkan can be seen slithering down the steps of El Castillo.
  • Chichen Itza was the capital of the Maya civilization for several centuries before its abandonment.
