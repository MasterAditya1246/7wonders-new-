The Great Wall of China

Introduction

The Great Wall of China is one of the most impressive and iconic structures in the world. Stretching over 13,000 miles, it was built to protect China from invaders. Today, it's one of the most visited landmarks, attracting millions of tourists every year.

History

Construction of the Great Wall began in the 7th century BC, but it wasn't until the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644) that the most well-known sections of the wall were built. The wall served as both a military defense and a symbol of China's strength and determination.

Fun Facts

  • The Great Wall is not a single continuous wall but a series of walls and fortifications built at different times.
  • It took more than 2,000 years to complete the Great Wall.
  • Some sections of the wall are made from earth, wood, and stone, depending on the region.
  • It is said that the wall is visible from space, though this is a myth.
