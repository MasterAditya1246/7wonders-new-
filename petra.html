Petra, Jordan

Introduction

Petra is a historical and archaeological city located in southern Jordan. It is famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system, which were carved into the rose-red sandstone cliffs. Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom in the 4th century BC and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

History

Petra was established as early as the 4th century BC by the Nabataeans, an Arab nomadic tribe. The city flourished due to its location on trade routes. The architectural feats of Petra, such as the famous Al-Khazneh (Treasury), are testaments to the ingenuity of the Nabataeans. Petra was rediscovered by Swiss explorer Johann Ludwig Burckhardt in 1812 after being lost to the Western world for centuries.

Fun Facts

  • Petra is known as the "Rose City" because of the color of its stone.
  • The Treasury (Al-Khazneh) was used as a filming location for the movie "Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade."
  • Petra remained unknown to the Western world until 1812, despite being a major city in ancient times.
  • The city contains over 800 monuments, including tombs, temples, and theaters.
