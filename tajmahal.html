Taj Mahal, India

Introduction

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an iconic symbol of love and one of the most famous monuments in the world. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is a stunning example of Mughal architecture. The Taj Mahal is renowned for its magnificent white marble dome, intricate carvings, and lush gardens, making it a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

History

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632, following the death of Mumtaz Mahal, and took approximately 22 years to complete, finishing in 1653. The monument is a blend of Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. The Taj Mahal complex includes a mosque, a guest house, and extensive gardens that represent the paradise described in the Quran. The mausoleum, with its perfectly symmetrical layout and grand proportions, is widely regarded as a masterpiece of architectural design.

Fun Facts

  • The Taj Mahal is made entirely of white marble, which was brought from various parts of India and Central Asia.
  • Over 20,000 artisans, craftsmen, and laborers worked on the Taj Mahal’s construction.
  • The monument changes color throughout the day, appearing pinkish in the morning, white at noon, and golden in the moonlight.
  • The Taj Mahal's gardens are laid out in the classic Persian style, representing paradise with four rivers flowing from the center.
  • Shah Jahan intended to build a black marble replica of the Taj Mahal for himself across the Yamuna River, but the plan was never realized.
