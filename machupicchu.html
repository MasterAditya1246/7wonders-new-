Machu Picchu, Peru

Introduction

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It was built in the 15th century by the Inca emperor Pachacuti and served as a royal estate. Rediscovered in 1911 by American explorer Hiram Bingham, it is now one of the most iconic archaeological sites in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

History

Machu Picchu was constructed by the Inca civilization around 1450, but it was abandoned in the early 16th century during the Spanish Conquest. The site remained largely unknown to the outside world until its rediscovery by Hiram Bingham in 1911. Despite its remote location, the city is a masterpiece of engineering, with its terraces, temples, and palaces built to harmonize with the natural environment.

Fun Facts

  • The name “Machu Picchu” means “Old Peak” in Quechua, the language of the Inca.
  • The site was never found by the Spanish, which is why it remained hidden for centuries.
  • It’s estimated that Machu Picchu was home to about 1,000 people during its peak.
  • The site is located at an altitude of about 7,970 feet (2,430 meters) above sea level.
